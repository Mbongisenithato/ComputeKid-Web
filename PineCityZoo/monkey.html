Overview

The **Dusky Leaf Monkey**, also known as the **Spectacled Langur** or **Spectacled Leaf Monkey** (*Trachypithecus obscurus*), is a species of primate belonging to the **Cercopithecidae family**. These monkeys are **native to Malaysia, Burma, and Thailand**, where they thrive in **tropical forests and dense jungles**.

Physical Characteristics

Dusky Leaf Monkeys are **easily recognizable** due to their **dark fur, white-rimmed eyes, and pinkish facial markings**, giving them a "spectacled" appearance. They have **long tails**, which help with **balance when navigating tree canopies**.

Diet & Habitat

These monkeys are **herbivorous**, feeding on **young leaves, fruits, and flowers**. They prefer **high treetop environments**, where they can evade predators and search for food efficiently.

Behavior & Social Structure

Dusky Leaf Monkeys are **highly social primates**, living in **groups of 5 to 20 individuals** led by a dominant male. They **communicate through vocalizations and facial expressions**, showing **strong maternal instincts and tight-knit community behaviors**.
