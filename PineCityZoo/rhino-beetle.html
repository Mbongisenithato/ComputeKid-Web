Overview

Rhino beetles belong to the **Dynastinae subfamily** of scarab beetles and are **one of the strongest insects in the world**. These beetles are **known for their impressive horn-like structures**, which resemble those of a rhinocerosâ€”hence their name.

Physical Characteristics

Rhino beetles are **large and robust**, with males displaying **distinctive curved horns** used for **competition and defense**. Despite their intimidating appearance, rhino beetles are **harmless to humans** and do not bite or sting.

Their **exoskeleton** is **incredibly tough**, providing protection against predators. Some species grow **up to 15 cm (6 in) long**, making them **among the largest beetles in the world**.

Strength & Behavior

One of the most astonishing facts about rhino beetles is their **incredible strength**â€”they can carry objects up to **850 times their own body weight**! This makes them **some of the strongest creatures on Earth** relative to their size.

Rhino beetles are primarily **nocturnal**, feeding on **fruit, sap, and decaying plant matter**. They are **excellent climbers**, using their powerful legs to grip surfaces securely.

Habitat & Conservation

These beetles are **found in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide**, including **Asia, Africa, and the Americas**. They play an **important ecological role**, aiding in **decomposition and nutrient recycling** in forest ecosystems.
