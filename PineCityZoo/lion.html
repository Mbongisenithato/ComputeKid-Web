Overview

The lion (*Panthera leo*) is one of the five big cats in the genus *Panthera* and a **member of the Felidae family**. The commonly used term **African lion** refers to **multiple subspecies found in Africa**. Some male lions **exceed 250 kg (550 lb) in weight**, making them the **second-largest living cat after the tiger**.

Historical & Current Range

Wild lions currently **exist in sub-Saharan Africa** and **Asia**, where an **endangered population resides in Gir Forest National Park, India**. Other lion populations **once roamed North Africa and Southwest Asia**, but **have since disappeared**.

Up until the **late Pleistocene epoch**, about **10,000 years ago**, lions were **the second-most widespread land mammal after humans**. They were found across most of Africa, **Eurasia from western Europe to India**, and even the **Americas, from the Yukon to Peru**.

Conservation Status

Lions are a **vulnerable species**, experiencing a **major population decline** across **30‚Äì50% of their African range every two decades** during the **second half of the twentieth century**. Their populations **cannot thrive outside designated reserves and national parks**, making conservation efforts crucial.

Although the **exact cause of their decline is unclear**, **habitat loss and human conflict** pose **significant threats**. Within Africa, the **West African lion population** is particularly endangered.
